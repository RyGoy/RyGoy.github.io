I miss Scott talking and explaining concepts. He will sit all night and try to get a point across if even one person is listening (not the same apparently as paying attention). Anyway, this is my (not Scott's) long-winded explanation as to why I see no value in him continuing the conference calls without a meaningful change in the way people view and participate in them. If anyone has a counter to this position, I'm interested to hear it.

As a super-deluxe bonus...the difference between "good" and "well". Good is an adjective. This means you correctly use it with a noun. Good job, good dog, good work, are all correct uses of the word. Well is an adverb, which means that it modifies verbs, adjectives, and other adverbs. Well, is well used in these situations, i.e. You speak English well.
